Visualizing Nvidia's Stock Performance and Trend

We have explored the performance of Nvidia's stock prices using autocorrelation. Autocorrelation is a statistical measure that determines the degree of similarity between a time series and a lagged version of itself over a certain time period. By calculating autocorrelation for Nvidia's stock prices, we aimed to identify any relationship between the stock's past performance and its future performance.

In this project, we have analyzed the stock market performance of three major companies, AMD, Nvidia, and Intel, using data visualization techniques and statistical procedures. We collected data on the stock prices of these companies over a period of several years. We explored various aspects of the market performance, including trends in the stock prices, correlation between the stock prices of different companies, and the impact of significant events on the market performance.
